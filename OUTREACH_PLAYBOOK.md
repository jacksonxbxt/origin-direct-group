## 5. What to Include in First Contact

### Always include:
1. **Who you are** — one sentence (Origin Direct Trading, West Africa commodity exporter)
2. **What you sell** — specific to their commodity (not a generic "we sell everything")
3. **Why you're different** — direct from origin, traceability, EUDR compliance, competitive pricing
4. **Social proof** — mention other markets you serve (without naming competitors)
5. **Low-commitment CTA** — sample, spec sheet, or 15-minute call

### If you have their import history (ImportYeti/Panjiva):
- "We noticed [Company] imported [X] shipments of [commodity] from [country] last year"
- "Your current sourcing from [origin] — we can offer comparable quality with [advantage]"
- This shows you've done homework and makes the email personal

### If you have their product line visible:
- "I see your [specific product] uses [ingredient] — we supply [grade] that would be a great fit"
- Reference their website, specific product names, certifications they promote

### Sample offer:
- Always offer to send a physical sample — it's the strongest conversion tool
- Cocoa: 2kg sample bag (fermented & dried)
- Cashew: 1kg sample of W240/W320
- Hookah charcoal: 1kg sample box
- Shea butter: 500g sample
- Include COA (Certificate of Analysis) with every sample

### Pricing:
- Don't put exact pricing in first email
- Say "competitive" or "direct-from-origin pricing"
- Share price ranges on a call or in follow-up after qualification
- Exception: hookah charcoal OEM — include $/kg range to qualify quickly
