## SECTION 6: OUTREACH STRATEGY NOTES

### For Cocoa (Craft/Bean-to-Bar)
1. **Best approach:** Send samples (fermented & dried only) with clear origin, processing notes, flavor profile, farm details
2. **Lead with:** Story of farmers, traceability, unique flavor notes, sustainability credentials
3. **Email subject lines:** "Fine Flavor Cocoa from [Origin] — Direct Trade Sample Available"
4. **Key hook:** EUDR compliance documentation (mandatory from 2025 for EU buyers)
5. **Timeline:** 6-24 months to close; be patient, follow up after ISM/Salon du Chocolat

### For Cocoa (Industrial/Netherlands-Belgium-Germany)
1. **Best approach:** Approach commodity traders first (Daarnhouwer, CocoaSupply, Tradin) before going to processors
2. **Must have:** EUDR-compliant supply chain documentation, sustainability certification (UTZ/RA/Fairtrade)
3. **Volume:** Must be able to deliver 20+ MT minimum per shipment

### For Cashew (Kernels — USA/EU)
1. **Best approach:** Provide quality specs sheet (grade, KOR, moisture, aflatoxin), certifications, price indication
2. **Must have:** HACCP, ISO 22000, or equivalent food safety cert; EU/US customs documentation
3. **Email pattern:** Most companies use info@, sales@, or procurement@ domains

### For RCN (India/Vietnam Processors)
1. **Best approach:** Price-competitive offer + quality documentation + origin story
2. **Contact via:** CEPCI directory (India), direct website contact (Vietnam), World Cashew Conference
3. **Key spec:** Outturn ratio (KOR) — processors primarily buy on KOR basis
4. **Timing:** March-July (African harvest season) is peak buying season
